GUIs lumber owner Gail and Kurt Villani killed in Rainbow Bridge Explosion near Niagara Falls

 Posted on May 13, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  421 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Grand Island, N.Y. – Tragedy struck on the Rainbow Bridge near the New York-Canadian border as a devastating car explosion claimed the lives of Gail Villani and Kurt Villani, the beloved owners of Gui’s Lumber Ace Hardware based in Grand Island, New York. The incident occurred on a bustling pre-Thanksgiving travel day, leaving authorities and the community in shock.

The couple, well-known for founding the family business in 1987, met an untimely demise in a fiery event that unfolded around 11:30 a.m. Reports indicated that a vehicle, traveling at high speed, collided, erupted in flames, and soared into the air, causing a catastrophic explosion on the bridge, which connects the United States and Canada.

Initial concerns about the incident being tied to terrorism were dispelled by the FBI, confirming no traces of explosive materials or terrorism-related activities. Governor Kathy Hochul, in a press conference, clarified that the tragic collision and explosion occurred on the U.S. side of the border and emphasized there was no indication of explosives at the scene.

The vehicular collision resulted in the fatalities of both occupants, one of whom was identified as a resident of western New York. Additionally, a booth agent sustained injuries in the incident but was treated and discharged from the hospital later in the day.

The chaos prompted the closure of bridge crossings from western New York into the United States initially, only to resume services after thorough assessments. However, the investigation poses challenges due to the extensive destruction of the vehicle, leaving debris scattered across 13 booths without a traceable license plate.

U.S. Secretary of Homeland Security Alejandro Mayorkas affirmed that there were no indications of terrorism, echoing the FBI’s findings. Furthermore, heightened security measures were adopted at various border crossings and transportation hubs as a precautionary measure.

The incident triggered responses from top officials, including President Joe Biden, who received briefings on the unfolding situation. Canadian Prime Minister Justin Trudeau assured that thorough investigations were underway, with additional security measures implemented at border crossings across the country.

Cities like Toronto and New York City reinforced security protocols in light of the incident, emphasizing caution and increased vigilance. Mayor Eric Adams confirmed enhanced security measures across entry and exit points in New York City, aligning with the heightened Thanksgiving security measures already in place.

As the investigation continues into this devastating event, the community mourns the loss of Gail Villani and Kurt Villani, pillars of their local business community, while authorities strive to unravel the circumstances surrounding this tragic car explosion.
